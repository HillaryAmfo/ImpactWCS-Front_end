Who We Are

WCS Impact is a global conservation organization that saves wildlife and wild places worldwide through science, conservation action, education, and by inspiring people to value nature.

Founded in 1895, WCS Impact has been a pioneer in wildlife conservation for over a century. Our mission is to conserve the world's largest wild places in 14 priority regions, home to more than 50% of the planet's biodiversity. We work in close partnership with local communities and governments to ensure sustainable solutions that benefit both nature and people.

Our work spans across diverse ecosystems, from vast savannas to pristine coral reefs.

What We Do

For over 125 years, WCS Impact has been at the forefront of conservation. Our work spans across diverse ecosystems, from the vast savannas of Africa to the pristine coral reefs of the Pacific. We protect iconic species like tigers, elephants, and marine mammals, and safeguard critical habitats essential for the planet's health.

Our scientific expertise informs our conservation strategies, ensuring that our efforts are effective and sustainable. We also focus heavily on environmental education, inspiring the next generation of conservationists and fostering a deeper connection between people and nature.

Our History & Milestones

1895: Founding of WCS Impact

Established by a group of visionary naturalists and philanthropists dedicated to protecting vanishing species.

1950s: First International Projects

Expanded conservation efforts to Africa and Asia, focusing on large mammal protection and anti-poaching initiatives.

1980s: Marine Conservation Focus

Launched major marine programs, including coral reef protection and whale research, recognizing the importance of ocean health.

2000s: Climate Change Integration

Integrated climate change impacts into all conservation strategies, developing adaptation and mitigation projects globally.
